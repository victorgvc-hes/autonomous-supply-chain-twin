[SLIDE 2: THE BUSINESS PROBLEM - 1 minute]

Let me start with the challenge many retailers face:

Imagine you're managing inventory for thousands of products. For many of them, 
60% of days have ZERO sales. Then suddenly, you might sell 4 units in one day. 
This is called "intermittent demand."

The business impact?
- Order too much → Money tied up in inventory, holding costs pile up
- Order too little → Empty shelves, lost sales, unhappy customers
- Traditional forecasting methods → Simply don't work for sparse data

Real example from our data: One product averaged 0.4 units per day, but 68% 
of days had zero sales. How do you plan inventory for that?

================================================================================

[SLIDE 3: OUR SOLUTION - 1 minute]

We built a three-part system:

PART 1 - SMART FORECASTING
Instead of guessing, we tested 8 different AI and statistical models on real 
Walmart data. We automatically select the best method for each product.

PART 2 - DIGITAL TWIN SIMULATION  
Before making real decisions, we simulate 90 days of operations. We can test 
"what if" scenarios: What if demand spikes? What if delivery is delayed?

PART 3 - AUTOMATED DECISIONS
The system recommends when to order and how much, balancing two costs:
- Holding inventory (storage, capital)
- Running out of stock (lost sales)

STOCKOUT RATE: Only 1.1%

COST EFFICIENCY: $271 total for 90 days

Here's the key insight: For low-volume products like these, SIMPLE approaches
